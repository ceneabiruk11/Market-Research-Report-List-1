<p><p>The dermatoscope market features two primary types: traditional and digital dermatoscopes. Traditional dermatoscopes use handheld devices with optical lenses to provide magnified views of skin lesions, enabling clinicians to assess conditions like melanoma. Digital dermatoscopes, on the other hand, incorporate advanced imaging technology to capture and analyze high-resolution images for further evaluation. They often include features like image storage and analysis software, enhancing diagnostic accuracy and enabling teledermatology practices, thereby broadening access to dermatological care.</p></p>
