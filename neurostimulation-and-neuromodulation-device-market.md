<p><p>Neurostimulation and neuromodulation devices are advanced medical technologies designed to alter the electrical activity in the nervous system, thereby treating a wide range of neurological disorders. Neurostimulation devices, such as deep brain stimulators and spinal cord stimulators, deliver targeted electrical impulses to specific brain regions or nerve pathways. In contrast, neuromodulation refers to methods that aim to adjust the activity of neural circuits, often through less invasive means like transcranial magnetic stimulation or bioelectronic medicine.</p><p>The Neurostimulation and Neuromodulation Device Market is witnessing significant growth driven by increasing prevalence of neurological disorders, rising demand for non-pharmacological treatment options, and technological advancements. Innovations in device design, miniaturization, and connectivity (such as remote monitoring) are enhancing patient adherence and outcomes. Additionally, the market is being influenced by a growing aging population, as older individuals often experience a higher incidence of conditions like chronic pain, epilepsy, and Parkinson’s disease. <p><p>Neurostimulation and neuromodulation devices are classified based on their placement and operation. Internal devices are surgically implanted within the body, delivering targeted stimulation directly to the nervous system, such as deep brain stimulators for managing conditions like Parkinson's disease. In contrast, external devices are worn on the body or used non-invasively to modulate nerve activity, such as transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) units. Both types aim to alleviate neurological disorders and improve overall patient outcomes.</p></p>
