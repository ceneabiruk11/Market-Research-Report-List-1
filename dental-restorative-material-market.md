<p><p>The dental restorative materials market encompasses various types, each designed for specific dental restorations. Amalgam is a durable, metallic material suitable for posterior teeth. Resin-based composites are aesthetic options that blend with natural teeth but may have lower strength. Glass ionomer releases fluoride and bonds to tooth structure, ideal for filling cavities. Resin-modified glass ionomer combines properties of both materials for improved strength and aesthetics. Porcelain-fused-to-metal offers a robust solution with the aesthetic appeal of porcelain, used in crowns and bridges.</p></p>
